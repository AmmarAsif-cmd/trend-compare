## Core Principles for Actionable Insights

### 1. **Answer the "So What?" Question**
Every insight must answer:
- Why does this matter?
- What should I do about it?
- When should I act?
- How can I benefit from knowing this?

### 2. **Be Specific, Not Generic**
âŒ Generic: "This trend is increasing"
âœ… Specific: "Search volume increased 340% on Day 1, sustained at +80% for 14 days, then declined to baseline by Day 45"

### 3. **Provide Timing Guidance**
Every insight should include:
- When did it happen? (past)
- When will it happen again? (prediction)
- When should you act? (recommendation)
- How long do you have? (opportunity window)

### 4. **Quantify Everything**
- Use numbers: percentages, multiples, days, dollars
- Show comparisons: before/after, vs competitor, vs baseline
- Calculate ROI when possible

### 5. **Make It Competitive**
- What are competitors doing?
- How can you differentiate?
- What's the white space opportunity?

### 6. **Segment by User Type**
Different users need different insights:
- **Marketers:** Content timing, keyword opportunities, budget allocation
- **Product Managers:** Launch timing, feature prioritization, competitive positioning
- **Investors:** Market timing, trend validation, risk assessment
- **Content Creators:** Topic selection, publishing schedule, SEO keywords

## Actionable Insight Categories

### Category 1: Pattern Recognition
**Purpose:** Identify recurring patterns for prediction

**Types:**
1. **Seasonal Patterns**
   - Annual cycles (e.g., "iPhone launches every September")
   - Quarterly patterns (e.g., "Earnings announcements Q1/Q2/Q3/Q4")
   - Monthly patterns (e.g., "New car sales peak at month-end")
   - Weekly patterns (e.g., "Netflix releases new shows on Fridays")

2. **Event-Driven Patterns**
   - Product launches (e.g., "Apple events trigger Android counter-announcements")
   - Industry conferences (e.g., "CES, MWC, Google I/O")
   - Sports events (e.g., "Super Bowl, World Cup")
   - Cultural events (e.g., "Black Friday, Prime Day")

3. **Lifecycle Patterns**
   - Hype cycle (e.g., "New tech trends peak 3 months after launch, then decline")
   - Adoption curve (e.g., "Early adopters â†’ mainstream â†’ late majority")
   - Decay patterns (e.g., "News stories peak on Day 1, half-life of 3 days")

### Category 3: Impact Quantification
**Purpose:** Measure the magnitude and duration of impact

**Metrics:**
1. **Search Volume Impact**
   - Peak magnitude (e.g., +340% on Day 1)
   - Sustained elevation (e.g., +80% for 14 days)
   - Time to baseline (e.g., 45 days)
   - Area under curve (total excess interest)

2. **Velocity Metrics**
   - Rate of increase (e.g., "10x spike in 6 hours")
   - Rate of decay (e.g., "50% decline every 5 days")
   - Acceleration (e.g., "Spike velocity increasing year-over-year")

3. **Comparative Metrics**
   - vs. previous year same event
   - vs. competitor announcements
   - vs. industry baseline

   - Contrarian takes (e.g., "Why I'm NOT upgrading to iPhone 15")
